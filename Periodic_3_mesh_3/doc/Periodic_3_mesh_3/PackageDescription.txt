By inserting well-chosen "dummy" points at the beginning of the meshing process,
the algorithm ensures that the triangulation forms a simplicial complex within
a single fundamental copy of the domain, at all times (see Section \ref P3Triangulation3secintro
of periodic triangulations). It is thus possible to safely construct a mesh
without duplicating vertices, and only the region of the input domain
that is included in in the original domain (see Section \ref P3Triangulation3secspace) is meshed.
The generation of this single copy of the periodic mesh is achieved using
the 3D mesh generator of the \ref PkgMesh_3Summary package.
Adapted oracles and criteria must be provided as to take the periodicity
of the input domain into account and to handle periodic elements.
This package offers this adaptation and its classes are consequently closely
related to those of the \ref PkgMesh_3Summary package.
